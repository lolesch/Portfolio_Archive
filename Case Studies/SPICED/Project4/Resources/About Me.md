## 1. “About me” section

<aside> 💡

Your "About Me" section is not your bio, it's a chance to show personality, clarity, and connection.

</aside>

You can structure your portfolio like a mini-story that would include the following:

### a. Who you are:

A sentence or two that frames your background in UX/UI and beyond.

<aside> 💡

**Example:**

“I'm a UX/UI designer with a background in psychology and a passion for human-centered design.”

</aside>

### b. What you care about

Your interests, values, or what motivates your work.

<aside> 💡

**Example:**

“I’m drawn to projects where design can reduce everyday frustration —

especially for older adults or people who aren’t tech-savvy.”

</aside>

### c. How you work

Talk briefly about your design approach or process.

<aside> 💡

**Example:** “I start with curiosity — asking questions, listening closely, and mapping what I hear. My process is grounded in research and synthesis. I find clarity by turning chaos into structure, then use prototyping to test assumptions early.”

</aside>

### d. Where you’re headed

A future-facing line that shows ambition and curiosity.

<aside> 💡

**Example:**

“Currently exploring the intersection between design and

AI-powered tools.”

</aside>

### About Me page: Do’s

- Write in first person (unless you truly prefer third)
- Keep it authentic and concise
- Use simple, friendly language
- Add a photo if you're comfortable — it humanizes your profile
- Mention soft skills or unique strengths

### About Me page: Don'ts

- Don’t repeat your CV word-for-word
- Don’t write an autobiography
- Use simple, friendly language
- Don’t overuse buzzwords without examples
- Don’t be afraid to show some personality!

# Portfolio Format - The PDF

## Why Create a PDF Portfolio?

- Some employers ask for it (especially in Germany & EU)
- It works great as an email attachment or leave-behind
- Good for those applying for remote jobs or freelance gigs that require attachments

## PDF Portfolio Essentials

You can include the following in your PDF portfolio:

- **Cover page:** Name, title, contact info
- **About me:** Short paragraph + photo (optional)
- **Table of contents** (if more than 3 projects)
- **2–3 strong case studies** (tailored to job type) Problem / Context / Process / Outcomes
- “CV Summary” section at the end Add a link to the full CV
- **Closing slide:** “Let’s connect” + contact info

## PDF Formatting Tips

- Use a consistent grid & layout
- Choose readable fonts (avoid overly decorative ones)
- Limit each case study to 6–8 slides/pages
- Keep file size under 10MB. Compress!
- Add clickable links (to website, LinkedIn, email)
- Remove any outdated / irrelevant projects
- Highlight your unique contribution on each team
